A monthly update of harassment and displacement risk in rent stabilized buildings in Brooklyn Community Board 12. Risk indicators include

  1. New sales of residential buildings
  2. Five or more new complaints filed with the Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD)
  3. Ten or more new violations issued by HPD
  4. Two or more new complaints filed with the Department of Buildings (DOB)
  5. New DOB construction permit applications.

Buildings that appear on multiple lists are coded yellow, orange, or red, based on the number of lists they're on.

Links let you click through directly to a property's Who Owns What page for owner info, HPD building info, DOB's Building Information System, ACRIS financial records, or property tax information. You can also click a building address to view it in Google Maps.

All buildings listed have registered stabilized units at least once between 2007 and 2016 (Notes).

If a building appears on more than one of the lists below in a given month it will be flagged:

Tip: to find the building on other lists, copy its BBL (Borough, Block, and Lot code) and use Find (Ctrl+F or ⌘+F)

Notes:

Rent stabilized unit numbers are approximate and based on property tax bills from the NYC Department of Finance: click a building's (Taxes) link for individual property tax bills. Missing or fluctuating numbers may be due to missing/incorrect registration by the owner or inconsistencies in property tax documentation. NYS Housing and Community Renewal maintains the most accurate information on rent stabilization, which is not available to the public.

The threshold of $50,000 is used to differentiate "true" or "arms-length" sales that reflect the property's market value from transfers between people or entitites with existing relationships.
